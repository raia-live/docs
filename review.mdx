## What is Review?

Review is SenseLab AI Agent engine for engineering teams that need to understand what AI is putting into their codebase before it ships. It adds AI-aware pull request analysis, risk scoring, and AIBOM generation on top of [AI Lineage](/ai-lineage) — giving engineering managers the full picture at every merge.

Where [AI Lineage](/ai-lineage) tells you _what happened_, Review tells you _what's about to happen_ — and whether you should be worried.

<Info>
  Review builds on top of [AI Lineage](/ai-lineage). You get everything in the free plan, plus PR-level intelligence.
</Info>

---

## The problem it solves

Code review was designed for humans reviewing human-written code. When AI writes the code, the dynamics break down:

- Developers are approving PRs that they can't fully explain
- Risk is invisible — there's no signal about which PRs carry the most exposure
- Nobody knows which packages and dependencies the AI silently added
- AIBOM generation happens after an incident, not before a merge
- There's no connection between what's in the PR and what it will do in production

Review closes these gaps before the merge button is clicked.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="A developer opens a PR">
    As soon as a pull request is opened, SenseLab begins analyzing it — attributing lines to models and developers, scanning dependencies, and computing the risk score.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review surfaces the risk">
    Reviewers see an AI-generated summary of what changed, who or what wrote it, which packages were added,  the risk score, and proposed changes, if any — directly in the PR context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AIBOM is generated">
    At review time, SenseLab generates an AIBOM for the PR. This becomes part of the evidence pack that follows the code through to release.
  </Step>
  <Step title="PR is linked to the release">
    Once merged, the PR is permanently linked to its release, deployment, approval, and comment history, as well as any subsequent production events. If something breaks, the trace goes all the way back.
  </Step>
</Steps>
